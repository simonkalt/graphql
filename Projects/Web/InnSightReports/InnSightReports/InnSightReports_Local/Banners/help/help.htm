Ban Man Pro Getting Started Guide

Ban Man Pro uses a very intuitive ABCZ approach to organizing Advertisers, Banners, Campaigns and Zones. 

Advertisers includes all information about the advertiser including: company name, description, web site, contact, contact's email, login name/password for viewing statistics, address, telephone, and fax information.

Banners are assigned to each advertiser.   An unlimited number of banners can be specified for each advertiser and this includes information such as:  banner ad description, target URL, image URL, text to display when mouse is hoovered over image, height, width, border, alignment, optional text to display underneath, and whether or not to launch a new instance of the browser when the banner is clicked.

Campaigns are simply a group of banners specific to an advertiser.  A campaign includes the following information:   campaign name, advertiser, included banners, banner weighting, start/end date, type of campaign (Flat Rate, CPM, or Per Click), quantity purchased by advertiser, cost, days of week to display campaign, daily start/end time, and the distribution (Front End --ASAP, or Even).

Zones are a group of campaigns.   Typically zones are created for the various pages throughout your web site.   For example, you might have a Homepage campaign, Product Page campaing, etc.   Multiple campaigns can be assigned to each zone and weighted differently.   Zone information includes description, page URL, mode (Server Side Include or HTML), and campaigns along with their weighting.

Follow these steps to begin working with Ban Man Pro:

  1. Upon successfully installing Ban Man Pro, setup the preferences.
  2. If using the Multi-site version of Ban Man Pro, add at least one site by selecting sites from the navigation bar at the bottom of the Ban Man Pro administration page.
  3. Next, add atleast one advertiser to the database.
  4. Add atleast one banner for this new advertiser.
  5. Now specify the campaign information for this advertiser and their associated banners.
  6. Group any campaigns you have entered into zones.
  7. Add the appropriate zone code to the appropriate page within your web site to begin rotating banner ads on that page.
